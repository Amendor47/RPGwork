The thesis in one move.

The European Union has not simply moved from norms to power. Since 2022, it has reframed power as necessary for the defence of norms: a coercive reinterpretation of the Kantian peace project under existential pressure.

01

Research question

How has the EU justified and institutionalised its post-2022 shift from a Kantian peace project to a geopolitical actor?

02

Main claim

The shift is neither pure betrayal nor pure realism: it is Active Kantianism, the defence of juridical peace through coercive instruments.

03

Normative test

The question is whether coercive capacity can remain legally constrained, democratically accountable and oriented toward peace.

The fight for a continent that is whole and at peace.

The coupling of “fight” and “peace” is no rhetorical accident. It names the thesis's central philosophical tension: can a peace project use coercive means without ceasing to be a peace project?
